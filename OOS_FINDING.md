# True out-of-sample result (2025-01 → 2026-06)

**Date:** 2026-06-15
**Test:** Extended `factor_model` and `earnings_model` configs to `end_date 2026-06-30`,
re-fetched all prices + EDGAR fundamentals/earnings, and evaluated the development
period (≤2024) against ~18 months of data that had never touched any config,
weight, or beta table in this repo. Generated by `oos_report.py` (split 2025-01-01).

This is the only test in the project that cannot be contaminated by
development-period peeking. The verdict:

## The two-sleeve book did NOT hold up out of sample.

| sleeve / book | dev Sharpe | dev total | **OOS Sharpe** | **OOS total** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| factor_vq (value+quality) | +0.62 | +37.2% | **+0.25** | **+2.6%** |
| pead (earnings drift)     | +0.29 | +19.7% | **−0.69** | **−4.7%** |
| combined (risk parity)    | +0.75 | +33.7% | **−0.05** | **−0.5%** |

The combined book that looked like Sharpe ~0.75–0.87 in development delivered
**roughly zero** (−0.5% total, Sharpe −0.05) across 18 true-OOS months.

## What broke, what survived

- **factor_vq survived, degraded.** OOS Sharpe +0.25 — about one standard error
  below its dev 0.62 (SE on an 18-month Sharpe ≈ ±0.82). Positive in 2025
  (+3.0%, Sharpe +0.40), flat in 2026 YTD (−0.3%, −0.04). Consistent with "real
  but weaker / regime-dependent value+quality," **not** with "broken." This is
  the only piece of the book with residual life.

- **pead broke.** OOS Sharpe −0.69, and *accelerating* the wrong way: −0.21 in
  2025, **−1.89 in 2026 YTD**. This is the textbook fate of PEAD — one of the
  most widely-published anomalies — as it gets crowded and prices incorporate
  earnings surprises faster. The standalone Sharpe was already only 0.29 in
  development; OOS it is a drag, not a diversifier.

- **The combiner machinery was not at fault.** OOS sleeve correlation held at
  −0.22 (negative, as designed). Vol stayed controlled (4.6% OOS) and the OOS
  drawdown was shallow (−4.4%). The book didn't blow up — it just made no money,
  because one of its two sleeves stopped working.

## Read the error bars

18 months → SE on the annualised Sharpe ≈ ±0.82. The combined OOS Sharpe of
−0.05 is ~1 SE below the dev 0.75: directionally a failure to replicate, not a
statistically overwhelming one. But the *economic* statement is unambiguous —
**the book earned ≈0% over a year and a half of live-equivalent data**, and the
deterioration is concentrated in the sleeve (PEAD) with the weakest prior and a
known crowding story. That is not noise you wait out; it is the result the test
was built to surface.

## Implications

1. **Do not size the two-sleeve book for deployment.** Its headline in-sample
   Sharpe did not survive contact with unseen data.
2. **factor_vq (value+quality) is the only thing still standing.** OOS +0.25 is
   modest and within error bars of its dev number — worth keeping on the bench
   and continuing to track, not worth betting on yet.
3. **PEAD is a documented decay, not bad luck.** Treat it as shelved alongside
   the insider (0.13/−0.01) and sector-rotation (0.03) candidates. The scoreboard
   of honest attempts now reads: factor_vq (marginal), pead (decayed), insider
   (dead), sector-rotation (dead).
4. The cheap lesson stands: we found this out for the price of compute, on data
   the models had never seen, instead of with capital.

## Reproduce

```
# configs already set to end_date 2026-06-30
cd factor_model   && python run.py        # ~16 min (full price + EDGAR refetch)
cd ../earnings_model && python run.py      # events cache now has a staleness guard
cd .. && python oos_report.py              # split @ 2025-01-01
```

Note: `earnings_model/events.py::build_events` gained a staleness guard (2026-06)
mirroring the factor-fundamentals one — without it, a cache built against an
older `end_date` would silently leave the OOS window with no earnings to trade
and fake a "flat" PEAD result. The fix forces an EDGAR rebuild when the newest
cached announcement lags the requested `end_date` by >90 days.
